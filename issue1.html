WORDS BY THE COLLECTIVE
"Why can't a girl have it all?"

Rachel hated coding growing up. She hated it because all she did was make pointless chatbots that asked for one's pizza preferences. That is, when she reached adulthood, she found out she could design anything she wanted using HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Starting with Freecodecamp's simple cat photo app exercise, she quickly realized that she did love coding because she loved design and storytelling. In fact, one of her favorite things to do is to make websites for others, conveying their personality and professionalism through the art of HTML files.

But even with her newfound happiness with coding, she wished she knew earlier that she could design with code. Disillusioned with the way coding was presented to young girls, she founded the Pink Coding Club, which embraced and empowered the "girly girl" and let her know she could code her way. Coding is an art form, and like with any other art form, coding requires creatives who can design, but with logic. This is why she believes a hyper-feminine perspective is essential in the tech world.

The disillionment she felt about the way coding and technology fields were marketed also fueled her to create the love, Rachel magazine. She realized it was not just coding that was marketed as unglamarous, but that STEM fields in general were marketed to be unappealing to young girls who longed for a life of glamour. By combining a high-fashion perspective with the stories of women in STEM, Rachel aims to change the way STEM is seen and challenge the stereotypes that surround a woman in STEM fields. Because... why can't a girl have it all?

INTERVIEW WITH A
VAMPIRE

(Software Engineer)

Naomi Carrigan is a transfemme software enginner committed to ethical technology development with a focus on inclusivity, sustainability, and community-driven innovation. She actively works to break down barriers in tech, support marginalized voices, and ensure her technologies respect user privacy while contributing positively to society. Her projects are open-source and community-centric.

THE COLLECTIVE

While you were learning to code, what made you realize “I want to do this for real”?

NAOMI CARRIGAN

Honestly? Nothing! I had finished the freeCodeCamp curriculum, and was still planning on going into human resources once my unemployment stipend was exhausted. I wanted to give back to freeCodeCamp because I very much believe in the mission, but I was not burning through my nest egg any faster than I had to. So I gave my time and skills instead - which ended up leading to my first job offer and my pathway into the industry. But it didn’t start to become “oh I wanna do this forever” until I was able to move in to my current home with my sister and her boyfriend. Having the ability to provide that sort of lifestyle? No way was I gonna give that up~

THE COLLECTIVE

Were there any times where you doubted your path in tech and wanted to give up? If so, how did you overcome that? And at what point in time did you feel this way?

NAOMI CARRIGAN

Honestly? No. I got super lucky in that my ADHD brain locked the heck in on software engineering. Still got that spark to this day~ But I do have advice! And it is probably unconventional advice. But Naomi’s secret to staying passionate about something? Be kind to yourself. Those moments where you doubt yourself? Where you think “I’m not cut out for this?” Where you wanna throw in the towel and resign yourself to a different path in life? Those are the moments where you need to give yourself the most grace. Those are the moments where your psyche, your body, your mind, they’re all telling you that you need a vacation. People will say “oh you just gotta power through” and like… I get where they’re coming from! Naomi very much believes that the goals worth your time are the ones that you pursue most relentlessly~ However! All of those down moments I mentioned earlier? You gotta listen to those! Because giving yourself the space to be human, to experience life, to pursue other passions, to feel things??? That’s part of pursuing your goals! If you do not take the rest you need, eventually you’ll be forced to rest. We call that burnout, your entire core just fizzles out and you have no desire to code. And it can last far longer than your rest~

"Be kind to yourself. "
